## 2. Objective

The objective of this room is to understand the fundamentals of computer networking, how devices communicate with each other, and why networking is essential in cybersecurity.

---

## 3. Introduction

Networking is the process of connecting two or more devices so they can communicate and share information. Networks enable computers, servers, mobile devices, and other systems to exchange data using standard communication protocols.

Understanding networking is one of the most important skills in cybersecurity because almost every cyber attack involves network communication.

---

## 4. Key Concepts

### What is a Network?

A network is a group of connected devices that communicate and share resources such as files, printers, internet access, and applications.

---

### Types of Networks

**LAN (Local Area Network)**

A LAN connects devices within a limited geographical area such as a home, office, or school.

**WAN (Wide Area Network)**

A WAN connects multiple LANs across large geographical distances. The Internet is the largest WAN.

---

### Internet

The Internet is a global network that connects millions of computers and networks around the world.

---

### IP Address

An IP (Internet Protocol) Address is a unique numerical identifier assigned to every device connected to a network.

Example:

- 192.168.1.10
- 8.8.8.8

---

### Router

A router connects different networks and forwards data packets between them.

---

### Switch

A switch connects multiple devices within the same local network and forwards data based on MAC addresses.

---

### Packet

A packet is a small unit of data transmitted over a network.

Large files are divided into multiple packets before being sent.

---

### Protocol

A protocol is a set of rules that defines how devices communicate over a network.

Examples:

- HTTP
- HTTPS
- DNS
- FTP
- SSH
- TCP
- UDP

## 7. Key Takeaways

- Networks allow devices to communicate and share information.
- Every device on a network requires a unique IP address.
- Routers connect different networks, while switches connect devices within the same network.
- Data travels across networks in the form of packets.
- Communication follows standardized network protocols.
