## 2. Objective

The objective of this room is to introduce the different career paths available in cybersecurity and explain the responsibilities, skills, and roles of various cybersecurity professionals.

---

## 3. Introduction

Cybersecurity is a rapidly growing field that offers a wide range of career opportunities. Professionals work to protect organizations, networks, systems, and sensitive information from cyber threats.

Different roles focus on different areas such as defending systems, testing security, investigating incidents, securing cloud environments, and ensuring compliance with security standards.

---

## 4. Key Concepts

### Security Analyst

A Security Analyst monitors networks and systems for suspicious activities, investigates security alerts, and helps protect an organization's digital assets.

### SOC Analyst

A Security Operations Center (SOC) Analyst continuously monitors security events, investigates alerts, performs incident analysis, and responds to cyber threats.

### Penetration Tester

A Penetration Tester (Ethical Hacker) identifies vulnerabilities by legally testing systems, networks, and applications before attackers can exploit them.

### Digital Forensics Analyst

A Digital Forensics Analyst collects, preserves, and analyzes digital evidence to investigate cyber incidents and support legal investigations.

### Incident Responder

An Incident Responder manages cybersecurity incidents by identifying, containing, eradicating, and recovering from security breaches.

### Security Engineer

A Security Engineer designs, implements, and maintains security solutions such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, VPNs, and endpoint protection.

### Cloud Security Engineer

A Cloud Security Engineer protects cloud environments by implementing secure configurations, identity management, data protection, and monitoring cloud resources.

### Malware Analyst

A Malware Analyst studies malicious software to understand its behavior, determine its impact, and develop detection methods.

## 7. Key Takeaways

- Cybersecurity offers multiple specialized career paths.
- Different roles require different technical and analytical skills.
- Continuous learning and hands-on practice are essential for career growth.
- Certifications, practical labs, and real-world experience help build a successful cybersecurity career.
- Communication and problem-solving skills are as important as technical knowledge.
