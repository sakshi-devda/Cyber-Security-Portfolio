# Network Topologies

## 📌 What is a Network Topology?

A Network Topology refers to the physical or logical arrangement of devices and connections within a network.

It defines how computers, servers, switches, routers, and other devices are connected and how data flows between them.

---

## 🎯 Why are Network Topologies Important?

Network topologies help:

- Improve network design.
- Simplify troubleshooting.
- Increase reliability.
- Optimize performance.
- Enhance scalability.

Choosing the right topology is important for building an efficient network.

---

# 🌐 Types of Network Topologies

## 1. Bus Topology

### What is Bus Topology?

In a Bus Topology, all devices are connected to a single communication cable called the backbone.

### Advantages

- Easy to install.
- Low cost.
- Requires less cable.

### Disadvantages

- Backbone failure affects the entire network.
- Difficult to troubleshoot.
- Performance decreases as devices increase.

### Example

Early Ethernet networks.

---

## 2. Star Topology

### What is Star Topology?

In a Star Topology, all devices connect to a central device such as a switch or hub.

### Advantages

- Easy troubleshooting.
- Better performance.
- Failure of one device does not affect others.

### Disadvantages

- Central device failure affects the entire network.
- Requires more cabling.

### Example

Modern office and home networks.

---

## 3. Ring Topology

### What is Ring Topology?

In a Ring Topology, devices are connected in a circular path.

Data travels from one device to the next until it reaches its destination.

### Advantages

- Predictable performance.
- Organized communication.

### Disadvantages

- One failure can disrupt the network.
- Difficult to expand.

---

## 4. Mesh Topology

### What is Mesh Topology?

In a Mesh Topology, devices are interconnected with multiple paths.

### Advantages

- High reliability.
- Excellent fault tolerance.
- Multiple communication paths.

### Disadvantages

- Expensive.
- Complex setup.

### Example

Large enterprise and critical infrastructure networks.

---

## 5. Tree Topology

### What is Tree Topology?

A Tree Topology combines multiple Star Topologies in a hierarchical structure.

### Advantages

- Easy expansion.
- Suitable for large networks.
- Structured management.

### Disadvantages

- Backbone failure impacts multiple segments.
- More complex than Star Topology.

---

## 6. Hybrid Topology

### What is Hybrid Topology?

A Hybrid Topology combines two or more network topologies.

### Advantages

- Flexible.
- Scalable.
- Can be optimized for different requirements.

### Disadvantages

- More expensive.
- Complex design and management.

---

# 📊 Topology Comparison

| Topology | Cost | Reliability | Scalability |

| Mesh | High | Very High | Medium |

## 🛡️ Network Topologies in Cybersecurity

Understanding network topologies helps security professionals:

- Identify network structures.
- Detect potential single points of failure.
- Improve network segmentation.
- Design secure architectures.
- Plan incident response strategies.

---

## 💼 Real-World Example

A corporate network typically uses a Star Topology:

- Employee computers connect to switches.
- Switches connect to routers.
- Routers connect to the Internet.

Large organizations may use Hybrid or Tree Topologies for better scalability.

---

## ⭐ Best Practices

- Use Star Topology for most modern networks.
- Avoid single points of failure.
- Document network layouts.
- Regularly review network design.
- Implement redundancy where possible.
