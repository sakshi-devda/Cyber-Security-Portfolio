# Phishing Attack

## 📌 Definition
Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or credit card details by pretending to be a trusted source.

---

## 🎯 Objective of Phishing
- Steal login credentials
- Gain financial information
- Install malware
- Gain unauthorized access

---

## 🧨 Types of Phishing

### 1. Email Phishing
Fake emails pretending to be banks, companies, or services.

**Example:**
“Your account will be locked. Click here to verify.”

---

### 2. Spear Phishing
Targeted phishing attack on a specific person or organization.

**Example:**
Fake HR email sent to employee.

---

### 3. Smishing (SMS Phishing)
Phishing through SMS messages.

**Example:**
“Your bank account is blocked. Click link.”

---

### 4. Vishing (Voice Phishing)
Phone calls pretending to be bank or support staff.

---

### 5. Clone Phishing
Copy of a real email but with malicious links replaced.

---

## ⚙️ How Phishing Works
1. Attacker creates fake message or website
2. Victim clicks link
3. Victim enters sensitive data
4. Attacker steals information

---

## 🛡️ Prevention
- Always check URL carefully
- Do not click unknown links
- Enable 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)
- Verify sender email address
- Avoid sharing OTP/passwords

---

## 🧠 Real-World Example
Fake bank emails asking users to “verify account” which redirect to cloned login pages.

---

## 🎯 Key Takeaway
Phishing attacks target humans, not systems. Awareness is the strongest defense.
