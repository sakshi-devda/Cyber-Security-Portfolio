# Scan Techniques

## 📌 What are Scan Techniques?

Scan techniques are different methods used by Nmap to gather information about a target system. Each technique sends different types of packets and analyzes the responses to determine whether ports are open, closed, or filtered.

Different scan techniques are useful in different situations depending on network configuration, firewall rules, and the level of stealth required.

---

# 🎯 Why are Scan Techniques Important?

Scan techniques help security professionals to:

- Discover open ports.
- Identify running services.
- Bypass simple firewall rules.
- Reduce detection by intrusion detection systems (IDS).
- Gather information efficiently.
- Perform accurate security assessments.

---

# 🌐 Common Scan Techniques

## 1. TCP Connect Scan (-sT)

The TCP Connect Scan completes the full TCP three-way handshake to determine whether a port is open.

### Advantages

- Reliable.
- Does not require raw packet privileges.

### Disadvantages

- Easier to detect.
- Generates more logs.

---

## 2. SYN Scan (-sS)

The SYN Scan is also known as the **Half-Open Scan**.

Instead of completing the TCP handshake, Nmap sends a SYN packet and analyzes the response.

### Advantages

- Faster than TCP Connect Scan.
- Less likely to be logged.
- Most commonly used scan.

### Disadvantages

- Usually requires root/administrator privileges.

---

## 3. UDP Scan (-sU)

Scans UDP ports to identify UDP services.

### Advantages

- Discovers UDP services.
- Useful for DNS, SNMP, DHCP, and NTP.

### Disadvantages

- Slower than TCP scanning.
- Responses are often limited.

---

## 4. FIN Scan (-sF)

Sends TCP FIN packets instead of SYN packets.

### Purpose

May bypass some simple firewalls.

---

## 5. NULL Scan (-sN)

Sends packets with no TCP flags set.

### Purpose

Can sometimes identify open ports while avoiding basic filtering.

---

## 6. Xmas Scan (-sX)

Sends packets with the FIN, PSH, and URG flags enabled.

### Purpose

Used to identify port states and bypass some firewall rules.

---

## 7. ACK Scan (-sA)

Used primarily to determine firewall rules rather than whether ports are open.

### Purpose

Helps identify whether ports are filtered or unfiltered.

---

## 8. Idle Scan (-sI)

Uses a third-party "zombie" host to perform a stealth scan.

### Advantages

- Very stealthy.
- Hides the attacker's IP address.

### Disadvantages

- More complex to configure.
- Requires a suitable idle host.

---

# 📊 Comparison of Scan Techniques

| Scan | Option | Speed | Stealth | Common Use |
|------|--------|-------|----------|------------|
| TCP Connect | `-sT` | Medium | Low | Basic scanning |
| SYN Scan | `-sS` | Fast | High | Default security scanning |
| UDP Scan | `-sU` | Slow | Medium | UDP services |
| FIN Scan | `-sF` | Medium | High | Firewall testing |
| NULL Scan | `-sN` | Medium | High | Firewall evasion |
| Xmas Scan | `-sX` | Medium | High | Firewall testing |
| ACK Scan | `-sA` | Fast | Medium | Firewall analysis |
| Idle Scan | `-sI` | Slow | Very High | Anonymous scanning |

# 🛡️ Scan Techniques in Cybersecurity

Security professionals use different scan techniques to:

- Assess network security.
- Detect exposed services.
- Test firewall configurations.
- Perform penetration testing.
- Conduct internal security audits.
- Support incident response investigations.

---

# 💼 Real-World Example

A penetration tester performs a SYN Scan on a web server to identify open ports without completing the TCP handshake. This approach reduces the amount of logged connection activity while still providing valuable information about available services.

---

# ⭐ Best Practices

- Obtain authorization before scanning.
- Choose the appropriate scan technique for the environment.
- Avoid aggressive scans on production systems.
- Analyze scan results carefully.
- Document all findings.
